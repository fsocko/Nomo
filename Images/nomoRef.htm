Some Reference Texts on Nomograms & Nomography



There is a considerable amount of literature on Nomograms and Nomography, much of it dating from the 1940's and 1950's. This was a time when the engineering disciplines were starting to undergo rapid change but with fairly limited calculational tools, that is, adding machines and slide rules. The demand for more rapid calculating power led to the design of custom nomograms. The books listed below seem to have been written by applied mathematicians and mechanical draftsmen. All emphasize methodologies for designing nomograms for highly specialized technical computing. The thing to notice about the design of nomograms is that issues such as (I) the ranges of the variables and (II) the choice of practical units for these variables are taken into consideration from the outset in the design process. It is regrettable that the same attention to these vital matters is not evident in the incredibly powerful, computer-based software of today.

H.J. Allcock and J.R. Jones, The Nomogram, 3rd ed., (London: Sir Isaac Pitman & Sons, Ltd.) 1946.

D.S. Davis, Nomography and Empirical Equations, (New York: Reinhold Publishing Corp.) 1955.
first half of book concerns empirical equations and correlations, geometric approach to charts.

D.S. Davis, Empirical Equations and Nomography, (New York: McGraw-Hill Book Co.) 1943.

R.D. Douglass and D.P. Adams, Elements of Nomography, (New York: McGraw-Hill Book Co.) 1947.
graphical approach, very methodical, avoids matrices and determinants.

M. Kraitchik, Alignment Charts: Construction and Use, (New York: D. Van Nostrand Co.) 1944.
nice basic treatment of mathematics of nomograms, uses determinant matrices throughout for generality.

L.E. Johnson, Nomography and Empirical Equations, (New York: John Wiley & Sons) 1952.
organized by equation type, scales are nicely explained, nice geometrical approach.
